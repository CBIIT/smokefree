Question 1/7
True or False? Smoking makes it harder for your body to heal from wounds.
That’s incorrect. Smoking restricts the amount of oxygen that reaches your tissues and makes it harder for your body to heal. This delay in wound healing can increase the risk of infections. Every try can help you quit for good.
True or False? Smokers are more likely to get gum disease and cancers of the mouth and throat.
That’s incorrect. Smoking can cause all kinds of health problems, including gum disease and mouth and throat cancer. The good news is quitting smoking can lower your risk for these diseases. Every time you put out a cigarette, it’s a new chance to try quitting again.
True or False? About 1 in 5 of all cancer deaths are caused by smoking.
Correct! Unfortunately, smoking causes about 1 in 3 cancer deaths in the US. Quitting smoking can lower your risk of getting cancer. It can take multiple tries to quit smoking for good. Don’t give up.
True or False? After 2 to 4 years of quitting smoking your risk for stroke is the same as people who have never smoked.
That’s incorrect. The sooner you quit, the sooner your body will begin to heal. With each quit attempt you learn more about what works for you and get closer to quitting for good.
True or False? Within 12 hours of quitting smoking the carbon monoxide level in your blood drops to normal.
That’s incorrect. The level of carbon monoxide in your blood drops to normal within 12 hours of quitting. Carbon monoxide in your blood is harmful because it deprives your heart, brain, and other vital organs of oxygen.
True or False? Within a few weeks of quitting, your risk of heart attack begins to drop.
That’s incorrect. Quitting smoking will improve your heart health. Your risk of a heart attack begins to drop within a few weeks of quitting. Even if you’ve already had a heart attack, you cut your risk of having another one by 30–50% if you quit smoking. If at first you don’t succeed, try, try again until you quit for good.
True or False? Smokers may get the flu more often than non-smokers.
That’s incorrect. Smokers have weaker immune systems, which may lead to more frequent bouts of flu and other respiratory infections.
